# Data Retention and Recordkeeping Policy

Document Type: Internal Policy
Effective Date: 2024-09-01
Version: 2.0
Region: United States
Supersedes: Version 1.4 (2022-03-10)
Owner: Data Governance Office

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes minimum and maximum retention periods for records generated during the debt collection lifecycle, ensuring compliance with FDCPA recordkeeping expectations, Regulation F documentation requirements, and applicable state statutes of limitation on debt collection actions.

## 2. Call Recordings

Call recordings related to collection activity must be retained for a minimum of 24 months from the date of the call. Recordings related to an account where a dispute, complaint, or litigation hold has been flagged must be retained for the duration of that hold plus an additional 24 months after resolution.

Call recordings must not be retained longer than 7 years from the date of the call unless subject to an active legal hold, after which they must be purged in accordance with the standard data destruction process.

## 3. Written Correspondence

All written correspondence with consumers, including validation notices, dispute responses, and hardship program correspondence, must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the account's closure or charge-off, whichever is later.

## 4. Case Management System Records

Case disposition logs, including call outcomes, disclosures given, and hardship indicators, must be retained for the life of the account plus 7 years.

## 5. Litigation Holds

Any record subject to a litigation hold notice from Legal must be retained indefinitely until the hold is formally released in writing by the Legal department, regardless of the standard retention period that would otherwise apply.

## 6. Data Destruction

Records reaching the end of their retention period must be destroyed using an approved secure destruction method (cryptographic erasure for digital records, certified shredding for physical records). Destruction must be logged with: record type, retention basis, destruction date, and method used.

## 7. Cross-Reference

For permitted use of recorded calls during active collection activity, see the Collections Call Practices Policy, Section 7 (Recordkeeping).
