# Hardship and Loss Mitigation Program Guide

Document Type: Program Guide
Effective Date: 2025-02-01
Version: 1.3
Region: United States
Supersedes: Version 1.2 (2024-04-01)
Owner: Loss Mitigation Department

## 1. Program Eligibility Overview

Consumers experiencing financial hardship (job loss, medical emergency, divorce, disability, or other documented hardship) may be eligible for one or more hardship programs. Eligibility is determined by the Loss Mitigation team based on documented income, hardship type, and account standing. Collectors on the front line must not promise eligibility or outcomes; only Loss Mitigation may approve program terms.

## 2. Available Programs and Fee Schedule

| Program | Eligibility Window | Term Length | Application Fee | Late Fee During Program |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Temporary Payment Reduction | Account 1-90 days past due | Up to 90 days | $0 | Waived |
| Loan Modification | Account 30-180 days past due | Permanent (re-amortized) | $0 | N/A (re-amortized) |
| Forbearance (secured products only) | Account up to 120 days past due | Up to 180 days | $0 | Waived |
| Settlement Offer | Account 90+ days past due, charge-off track | One-time | $0 | N/A |

Note: As of Version 1.3, all application fees were waived program-wide; prior versions (1.2 and earlier) charged a $25 application fee for Loan Modification review. Collectors referencing older training material should confirm current fee status against this document, not memory.

## 3. Documentation Required

For all hardship program applications, the consumer must provide:
- A signed hardship affidavit
- Proof of income (or proof of income loss, where applicable)
- For medical hardship: documentation from a licensed provider (diagnosis not required, only confirmation of treatment/incapacity)

## 4. Temporary Payment Reduction — Details

This program reduces the minimum payment due by up to 50% for a period not exceeding 90 days. At the end of the 90-day period, the account returns to standard payment terms; any reduced amount is not forgiven and is re-amortized across the remaining loan term unless the consumer qualifies for and elects a subsequent program.

## 5. Loan Modification — Details

A permanent change to loan terms (interest rate, term length, or both) intended to bring the monthly payment to an affordable level based on documented income. Requires full underwriting review by Loss Mitigation. Average review time is 10-15 business days from receipt of complete documentation.

## 6. Forbearance — Details

Available only for secured products (mortgage, auto). Suspends or reduces payments for up to 180 days. Interest continues to accrue during the forbearance period unless otherwise stated in the consumer's specific approval letter. At the end of the forbearance period, the consumer must either resume standard payments with a repayment plan for the deferred amount, or transition to a Loan Modification if still unable to resume standard payments.

## 7. Settlement Offers

For accounts on the charge-off track (typically 90+ days past due with no active hardship program), Loss Mitigation may, at its discretion, offer a one-time settlement for less than the full balance owed. Settlement offers are not guaranteed and are evaluated case-by-case based on account value, age, and recovery likelihood. Any settlement agreement must be documented in writing and signed by the consumer before being considered final.

## 8. Referral Process

Front-line collectors identifying a hardship indicator during a call should:
1. Acknowledge the hardship without making promises about outcomes
2. Offer to transfer the call to Loss Mitigation, or provide the dedicated contact number
3. Log the hardship indicator in the case management system per the Collections Call Practices Policy, Section 7
