# Collections Call Practices Policy

Document Type: Internal Policy
Effective Date: 2025-01-01
Version: 3.2
Region: United States
Supersedes: Version 3.1 (2023-06-15)
Owner: Collections Compliance Department

## 1. Permitted Calling Hours

Collectors may contact a consumer regarding a debt only between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM in the consumer's local time zone, as required under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), 15 U.S.C. § 1692c(a)(1). If the consumer's time zone cannot be determined with confidence, the time zone of the address on file at account origination must be used as the default.

Calls placed outside this window, even due to a system error, must be logged as a compliance exception within one business day and escalated to the Compliance Desk.

## 2. Required Disclosures

Every initial communication with a consumer regarding collection of a debt must include the following disclosure, commonly referred to as the "mini-Miranda" warning:

"This is an attempt to collect a debt. Any information obtained will be used for that purpose."

This disclosure is required under FDCPA § 1692e(11). The disclosure must be given in the initial communication and, separately, in the initial written communication to the consumer, if the initial communication is oral.

Within five days of the initial communication, the consumer must receive a written validation notice containing:
- The amount of the debt
- The name of the creditor to whom the debt is owed
- A statement that the consumer has 30 days to dispute the debt, and that the collector will assume the debt is valid if not disputed within that period
- A statement that, if the consumer disputes the debt in writing within the 30-day period, the collector will obtain and mail verification of the debt

## 3. Cease and Desist Requests

If a consumer requests in writing that the collector cease further communication, the collector must comply, except to:
- Advise the consumer that further collection efforts are being terminated
- Notify the consumer that the creditor may invoke specified remedies (e.g., legal action)
- Notify the consumer that the creditor intends to invoke a specified remedy

A verbal cease-and-desist request should be honored as a best practice and escalated for written confirmation, but the formal FDCPA protection applies specifically to written requests.

## 4. Prohibited Contact Methods and Frequency

Collectors must not contact a consumer at their place of employment if the collector knows or has reason to know the employer prohibits such communication.

Collectors must not call a consumer's phone number more than seven times within a seven-day period, or place a call to a consumer within seven days after engaging in a telephone conversation with the consumer regarding the same debt, per Regulation F (12 CFR Part 1006) implementing the FDCPA.

## 5. Third-Party Contact Restrictions

Collectors may contact third parties (excluding the consumer's attorney, if represented) only to obtain location information about the consumer, and such contact is limited to one occasion unless the collector reasonably believes the prior information was incorrect or incomplete. Collectors must not reveal that the consumer owes a debt when contacting third parties for location information.

## 6. Hardship and Loss Mitigation Referral

If a consumer indicates financial hardship during a collections call, the collector must offer to transfer the consumer to the Loss Mitigation team or provide the dedicated Loss Mitigation contact number. Hardship programs available include:
- Temporary payment reduction (up to 90 days)
- Loan modification review
- Forbearance (for qualifying secured products)

Collectors must not independently promise specific hardship outcomes; only the Loss Mitigation team is authorized to approve hardship program terms.

## 7. Recordkeeping

All collection calls must be recorded and retained per the Data Retention Policy (see separate document). Call dispositions must be logged in the case management system within the same business day, including: outcome, any disclosures given, any disputes raised, and any hardship indicators noted.
